Manny Pacquiao Biography

Manny Pacquiao was born on December 17, 1978, making him 44 years old. He was born in Kibawe, Bukidnon, Philippines, and is a Sagittarius. Emmanuel “Manny” Dapidran Pacquiao Sr. is his full name.

Manny’s parents divorced when he was in sixth grade. It was because his father was having an affair. Manny, on the other hand, has five siblings, of which he is the fourth. Alberto “Bobby” Pacquiao is one of his siblings. Manny’s brother is a politician as well as a former professional boxer.

Manny received his elementary education at Saavedra Saway Elementary School in General Santos City. Manny, however, dropped out of high school due to extreme poverty.

Sardo Mejia, Manny’s maternal uncle, introduced him to boxing when he was 12 years old. Manny stated in his autobiography that witnessing Mike Tyson’s defeat to James “Buster” Douglas in 1990 changed his life forever. Furthermore, Bruce Lee, the martial artist, and Muhammad Ali, the boxer, influenced Manny’s early interest in combat sports.

Manny Pacquiao Education

In terms of education, Manny began training with his uncle in a makeshift home gym in 1990. Manny began boxing in a park in General Santos after six months of training. He eventually moved on to fight in other cities against higher-ranked opponents. Manny had become the best junior boxer in the southern Philippines by the age of 15 years. He moved to Manila at the same age.

Manny made his professional boxing debut in January 1995. He made his junior flyweight debut when he was only 16 years old. Manny also passed a high school equivalency exam in February 2007. Moreover, Manny received a high school diploma as a result of this achievement from the Department of Education.

Manny later attended Notre Dame of Dadiangas University and the University of Makati. Manny earned a bachelor’s degree in political science from the latter. He majored in local government administration through the Philippine Councilors League-Legislative Academy’s Expanded Tertiary Education Equivalency and Accreditation Program (ETEEAP) (PCCLA).

Manny graduated from Makati on December 11, 2019, completing his collegiate education through the informal education system.

Furthermore, on February 18, 2009, at the Waterfront Cebu City Hotel & Casino in Lahug, Cebu City, Southwestern University (SWU) conferred Manny with the Honorary Degree of Doctor of Humanities (Honoris Causa). Manny’s boxing accomplishments and humanitarian work were recognized by the university.

Manny Pacquiao Professional Life

Manny Pacquiao is a well-known former boxer and politician. He is known as “PacMan” and is widely regarded as one of the greatest professional boxers of all time. The Filipino boxer is currently a Senator of the Philippines and the party president of the ruling PDP–Laban party.

Manny has also won 12 major world titles and is the only eight-division world champion in boxing history. In addition, the professional boxer was the first to win the lineal championship in five different weight classes. In addition, he was the first boxer to win major world titles in four of the eight glamour divisions. Flyweight, featherweight, lightweight, and welterweight are the glamorous divisions.

Manny is also the only boxer to have held world titles in four decades, from the 1990s to the 2020s. Manny’s fights had generated $1.2 billion in revenue by 2015. It was from one of his 25 pay-per-view fights. In the same year, Forbes ranks Manny as the world’s second-highest-paid athlete.

Similarly, Manny became the oldest welterweight world champion in history in July 2019. At the time, he was 40 years old. He also became the first boxer in history to be a four-time welterweight champion. Manny won the WBA (Super) welterweight title that year after defeating boxer Keith Thurman.

Manny has defeated 22 world champions in his boxing career. Among the champions Manny has defeated over the years are Chatchai Sasakul, Lehlohonolo Ledwaba, Adrien Broner, and Keith Thurman, to name a few. Manny has also twice defeated Marco Antonio Barrera, Érik Morales, Juan Manuel Márquez, and Timothy Bradley.

More On Career

Manny was named “Fighter of the Decade” for the 2000s by the Boxing Writers Association of America (BWAA), the World Boxing Council (WBC), and the World Boxing Organization (WBO). Similarly, Ring magazine, ESPN, and the BWAA named him “Fighter of the Year” in 2006, 2008, and 2009.

Manny also received the “Best Fighter ESPY Award” in 2009 and 2011. Manny is also regarded as the greatest Asian fighter of all time by BoxRec. Moreover, Manny was also ranked second and fifth on ESPN’s and BoxRec’s lists of the top pound-for-pound boxers of the last 25 years, respectively, in 2016.

Similarly, the professional boxer has been a member of the Premier Boxing Champions (PBC) promotion since 2018. He is also under contract with Paradigm Sports through 2020. Manny has also been the WBA welterweight champion-in-recess since January 2021.
